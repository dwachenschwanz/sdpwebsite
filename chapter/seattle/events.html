The Rules of Actional Thought

Dr Steven Tani, Strategic Decisions Group (SDG) Fellow and member of the board of directors, and SDP Fellow, discusses the theoretical foundation of decision analysis, as formulated by Professor Ron Howard, much as Euclid’s axioms provide the foundation for the study of geometry.  The Five Rules of Actional Thought

Chapter December 2020 Meeting

Two Sides of a Coin - The Science and the Human in the Loop 

A conversation where both the so-called soft-side of decisions (the human in the loop, cognition) and the hard science/modeling (decision science) are both seen as necessary contributors and complementary to the outcome.

Presenter/Facilitator: Dr. John R. Black, President of Aragon National Inc., a consulting business specializing in insight generation and related research. A doctoral researcher in the area of data visualization and critical thinking skills, John is currently exploring how sensemaking and complexity affects decision-making in cultures such as law enforcement and the military. John will be using excerpts from his two presentations to the Society for Police and Criminal Psychologists (SPCP) given this month.

Chapter October 2020 Meeting

Superforecasting

Making forecasts is critical to good decision-making. In his 2015 book, Superforecasting: The Art and Science of Prediction, Philip Tetlock describes how he created a team of "superforecasters" that beat intelligence professionals in their field of making predictions for world events.

7-8 PM PT

Chapter September 2020 Meeting

Virtual tools for remote DQ engagement

Even before the pandemic required us to work remotely, the tools and platforms available for remote engagement were improving and becoming more widely used. Helping groups reach a high level of decision quality is challenging even when we're in the same room. But with the right tools and right approaches, remote engagement can be more effective rather than less. Troy will demonstrate some of the tools and approaches that SDG uses, and then we’ll discuss alternatives and best practices that others have tried or experienced.

Troy Helm, Strategic Decisions Group

Location: Online

Projects and Gamification Make Learning Fun

Never Underestimate the power of play! Research shows group games create community, increase retention, and build engagement all while having fun - at any age. Let's watch a movie about decision making and play a game. Bring your own popcorn and favorite beverage!
